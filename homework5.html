Homework #5: Vector Emotive Eye & Illustrated Enviorment



-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


For this enviorment I choose to use a picture of one of my plushies looking out of the window in my apartment complex. I do like the use of Trindad colors for this excercise because The main object of focus here is able to stand out more effectively. And as you move past the plush, the colors gradually become less colors and more warmer in tone. So although the color palette I chose did limit how my enviorment could be portrayed --such as how in the original piece its daytime and there are less softer colors in my palette-- The colors are still unqiue enough to seperate the three layers of the piece. The foreground is the plushie, with the most cooler colors and more saturation. Following, the shadow and window seal starts to fade away from that color and act as a middleground between the plush and outside-- along with the shine of the window thats supposed to be portrayed by the white squares. Lastly, the background consists of a source of light and a slight hint of buildings behind it. Together I shaped the piece to be more cartoony and mostly focusing on the main subject at hand.
